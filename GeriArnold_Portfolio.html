About

Human-centered
by training.

My path into UX is unusual — and that is the point. A foundation in Industrial Design taught me to think about the whole artifact: how it sits in a hand, how it lives in space, how it fails gracefully. A Master's in Human Factors and Cognitive Engineering gave me the science to understand why people err and how systems can be built to prevent it.

That combination — aesthetic maker plus analytical engineer — is what I bring to every screen, workflow, and research study. I currently teach Human-Centered Design at Cuyahoga Community College, bringing this practice to the next generation.
